Combinations & Permutations

Combinations & Permutations

Counting principles, factorial notation, permutations, combinations, and arrangement problems.

Created: December 15, 2025

Overview

  • Counting arrangements and selections — decide whether order matters.
  • Use factorials to compute permutations and combinations.

Factorial

$$ n! = n \times (n-1) \times (n-2) \times \dots \times 1 \quad (0! = 1) $$

Permutations (order matters)

$$ P(n,k) = \dfrac{n!}{(n-k)!} $$

Example: From A,B,C,D make 3-letter arrangements: P(4,3)=4!/(1!)=24.

Combinations (order does not matter)

$$ \binom{n}{k} = \dfrac{n!}{k!\,(n-k)!} $$

Example: Choose 2 from 4: C(4,2)=6 (AB, AC, AD, BC, BD, CD).

Quick rule

  • If order matters, use permutations; otherwise use combinations.
  • Relation: P(n,k) = C(n,k) × k! — permutations count each combination in k! orders.
Derivations (click to expand)

This section gives concise derivations for the permutation and combination formulas.

Permutation formula

To form an ordered list of length k from n distinct items: choose the first item in n ways, the second in n-1 ways, and so on until the k-th item in n-k+1 ways. Multiply these choices:

$$ P(n,k) = n (n-1) (n-2) \cdots (n-k+1) = \dfrac{n!}{(n-k)!} $$
Combination formula

Each unordered selection of k items corresponds to k! different ordered lists (permutations). Therefore divide permutations by k!:

$$ \binom{n}{k} = \dfrac{P(n,k)}{k!} = \dfrac{n!}{k!\,(n-k)!} $$
